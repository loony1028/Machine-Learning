Healthcare Stroke Prediction Analysis
-

context
-

According to World Health Organization (WHO) stroke is the 2nd leading cause of death globally, responsible for approximately 11% of total deaths.

The dataset is used to predict whether a patient is likely to get stroke based on the input parameters like gender, age, various diseases and smoking status. Each row in the data provides relevant information about the patient.

Attribute Information:

1. id: unique identifier
2. gender: "Male", "Female", "Other"
3. age: age of patient
4. hypertension: 0 if the patient doesn't have hypertension, 1 if the patient have hypertension
5. heart_disease: 0 if the patient doesn't have any heart diseases, 1 if the patient have a heart disease
6. ever_married: "No" or "Yes"
7. work_type: "Children", 'Govt_job", "Never_worked", "Private", "Self-Employed"
8. Residence_type: "Rural" or 'Urban"
9. bmi: body mass index (determine if someone has the appropriate size or weigh for their height and other factors)
10. avg_glucose_level: average glucose level in blood
11. smoking_status: "formerly smoked", "never smoked", "smokes" or "Unknown"
12. stroke: 1 if the patient have a stroke and 0 if not

** Note: "Unknown" in smoking_status means that the information is unavailable for the patient
